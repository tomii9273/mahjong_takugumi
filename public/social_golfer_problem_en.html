Solutions of Social Golfer Problem

Some of the match tables on this website are solutions to the Social Golfer Problem (SGP), a combinatorial design problem in discrete mathematics.
Summary of SGP: "g×p golfers play golf once a week in g groups of p golfers each. Is it possible to set up a schedule of w weeks without any two golfers playing together in a group more than once?"
The solution to the problem and the match table correspondence are summarized on this page.

variable character name at SGP name at mahjong match tables
g number of groups number of tables (卓数)
p number of players (golfers) in a group p-player mahjong (p 人麻雀)
w number of weeks number of matches (対戦数, 戦数)

If g and p are fixed, it can be shown that the maximum value of w does not exceed (gp-1) / (p-1).
If the maximum w is achieved in that case, a circle is placed in the w-max column.

proof

Consider the position of a certain player. The number of players he can play with in a single round is p-1.
If players other than the player (total gp-1 players) are assigned to the same table one match at a time, the number of players will be insufficient after (gp-1) / (p-1) rounds.
Therefore, the maximum number of matches w does not exceed (gp-1) / (p-1).
